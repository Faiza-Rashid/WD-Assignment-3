Most Wonderful Flowers

  • Rose
    • Why Roses are famous?


    Roses have a long and colorful history. They have been symbols of love, beauty, war, and politics. The rose is, according to fossil evidence, 35 million years old. In nature, the genus Rosa has some 150 species spread throughout the Northern Hemisphere, from Alaska to Mexico and including northern Africa. Garden cultivation of roses began some 5,000 years ago, probably in China. During the Roman period, roses were grown extensively in the Middle East. They were used as confetti at celebrations, for medicinal purposes, and as a source of perfume.
  • Marigold
    • What is special about Marigold?


    Marigolds do not thrive during winter since they are sensitive to cold and frost. Marigold flowers have lutein which is good for our eyes. Marigold flowers have antibacterial and antiviral substances that give us health benefits. Marigold flowers are used in beauty, warmth and creativity.
  • Dahlia
    • Why is it called Dahlia?


    Many sources state that the name "Dahlia" was bestowed by the pioneering Swedish botanist and taxonomist Carl Linnaeus to honor his late student, Anders Dahl, author of Observationes Botanicae.
